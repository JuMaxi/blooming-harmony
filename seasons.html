January - Plan with Purpose

Start the year by planning your garden with sustainability in mind — choose native plants, rotate crops, and consider water-saving layouts.

Read More

February - Start Seeds Indoors

Reduce plastic use by sowing seeds in reused containers like egg cartons or yogurt pots. It’s eco-friendly and gets you ahead for spring.

Read More
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 

March – Prepare Soil Organically

Enrich your soil with homemade compost or well-rotted manure. Avoid chemical fertilizers to support biodiversity below ground.

Read More

April – Attract Pollinators

Plant nectar-rich flowers and avoid pesticides to create a haven for bees and butterflies as they return in spring.

Read More
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 

May – Mulch for Moisture

Add a layer of mulch around plants to retain moisture, suppress weeds, and reduce watering needs — naturally.

Read More

June – Harvest Rainwater

Install a water butt or rain barrel to collect rainwater for your garden. It saves water and keeps your plants happy.

Read More
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 

July – Shade and Shelter

Use taller plants or recycled materials to create shade for heat-sensitive crops, reducing sun damage and water evaporation.

Read More

August – Sow for Autumn

Sow late-season crops like spinach, carrots, and kale. Extend your harvest while making the most of summer warmth.

Read More
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 

September – Save Seeds

Collect seeds from healthy plants to replant next year. It’s cost-effective, sustainable, and promotes biodiversity.

Read More

October – Compost Leaves

Rake fallen leaves into a compost bin or pile. They break down into leaf mould, a rich soil conditioner.

Read More
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 

November – Protect Wildlife

Leave some garden areas undisturbed — they offer shelter to hibernating insects, frogs, and hedgehogs.

Read More

December – Decorate Naturally

Use foraged pinecones, branches, and holly for decorations instead of plastic — garden-friendly and festive!
